October 3rd 1890.

The North American Review.

3 East Fourteenth Street, New York.1



Dear Sir:—

Can you write a brief article for the North American Review on Recent Aspects of American Literature as you have observed them? It need not be more than 4000 words in length—about ten pages of the Review—and in return for it we should be glad to place at your disposal the sum of Two hundred dollars. Or possibly there is some other subject on which

you would be more willing to write. In that case we trust you will allows us an opportunity to consider it.2

I am, dear Sir,

Faithfully Yours,

William H. Rideing,

assistant editor of the Review.

Correspondent:
As yet we have no information about this correspondent.
