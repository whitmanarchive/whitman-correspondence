June 21st, 1874 Wm. H. Taylor

201 [ Elm?] street

Newark, N.Y.



My Dear Friend

Your Letters & papers came early at hand. But I did not know that you had been sick until I received your Letter; But I hope you are improving, and will finally get all right again.

I left New York in Oct. 1868 and me and my Brother started [illegible] [ separated?] and I commenced to Paddle my own Canoe. I have A small route And keep A Dep't. And am making A fortune very Slow indeed, so slow—that I shall never get rich;

And all the rest of us are in [illegible] usual good Health. I should very much like to come and see you But my time is [ pretty much?] Occupied. But should very much like you come and see me at any time you feel able.
