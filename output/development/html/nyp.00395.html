Dover, Delaware, February 25, 1875



Beloved Friend,

Your Letter & Picture & Paper came safe. I hope you will excuse me for not writing sooner I have been laid up with a pain in my back ever since tuesday last, but I am feeling better at this present time & hope to [be] able to go work tomorrow [morn]ing. I cant find words to tell you how thankful I am for the Picture. I dont think that I ever had a present that I think more of than I do it. I am agoing to have it framed if nothing happens               I want that Picture to remain in my family as long as thier is a member of it living. I would like very much to see you & have a long talk with you & if nothing happens I want you to visit us soon as the Weather gets pleasanter. We are as comfortable as the most of poor people that has to labor for a living. this is a very plaesent place in the summer time when the trees are all out in full bearing.

I will stop hopeing this may find you improving in health.. also

with much love & many thanks from myself & my Wife

Correspondent:
