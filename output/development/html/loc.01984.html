535, 15th St Wash
Oct 6th/74



Mr. Whitman

Dear Sir

I regret very much that I am obliged to write to you again, but as I am very much in need of some money just as soon as possible I thought perhaps you would be willing to settle up your bill if you do you will greatly oblige me we have nearly two hundred & fifty dollars besides the regular expenses to pay within the next twenty days so you will see that we will need all that we can make or get that is due us. Your bill was up to the first of August thirty eight dollars ($38.00) I am willing to give you ten dollars ($10.00) for the things which I took from you that is nearly as much as they would cost if new and as much as they are worth to me. Then you will still owe twenty eight ($28.00) which if you could let me have this week will be a great favorâ€”

Of course I will not charge you for keeping your things since, but I would be glad if you would have them removed soon

Yours Respectfully

Mrs Isabella A White

Correspondent:
Walt Whitman stayed with Dr. George A. White, a chiropodist, and his wife Isabella A. White from March 1, 1871, until Whitman left Washington. Whitman had paid $236 in rent through June 10, 1873 (The Library of Congress #73). On November 28, 1873, Dr. acknowledged for his wife receipt of $28 "on account . . . for rent of room etc from May 1st/73" (Charles E. Feinberg Collection). Walt Whitman gave up one room at the Whites' on June 10, 1873: "Kept the other at $2.50 a month" (The Library of Congress #68). According to a letter dated , Isabella White had written, evidently early in July, about the rent due for his room; Whitman's reply is not extant. On the 29th, White offered to purchase Walt Whitman's bedstead and certain other effects. Walt Whitman had not settled his account when White wrote again on , and offered him a credit of $10 for his furnishings against a balance of $38. These letters are in the Charles E. Feinberg Collection.(See also Walt Whitman's , letter to Peter Doyle, in which Whitman left instructions for the delivery of his boxes from the Whites.)
