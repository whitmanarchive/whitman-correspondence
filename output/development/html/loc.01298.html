White Hall, Ky.
Jan. 6. 18911



Dear Sir,

I have just received your "Leaves of Grass &c." 1890—for which accept my thanks.

I have not found time but to glance over it & cannot return a criticism—even if such a thing was a consequence.

I am very independent in such matters—and think with Burns

"Crooning to a body's sel

Does well enough"2

& let the world read or not, as it likes.

—Wishing you long years yet of health and happiness I remain yours truly

Cassius Marcellus Clay

Correspondent:
Cassius Marcellus Clay (1810–1903), often called "Lion of the White Hall," was an abolitionist polititian from Kentucky and American diplomat to Russia during the Lincoln administration.
