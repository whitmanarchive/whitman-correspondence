Correspondent:
The New York Herald (1835–1924), a daily newspaper, became one of the longest running newspapers. The Herald was founded by James Gordon Bennett, Sr., and his son, James Gordon Bennett, Jr (1841–1918) was the editor and publisher of the Herald, during the time Whitman published poems and prose pieces in the paper. Whitman published his largest number of periodical poems in the Herald. For more information on the paper and Whitman's contributions, see Susan Belasco, "New York Herald."

Notes:

1. This letter is a draft. It is endorsed: "bill to Herald—$100 | sent March 1, '88 | Mr Browning (Phila. representative Herald) here March 7 '88."[back]

2. Apparently, the editors of the New York Herald sent a note to Whitman on February 19, 1888, requesting a bill for his recent contributions. This draft represents his first effort. A finalized list was sent later in the day, and the Philadelphia representative of the Herald brought Whitman his payment on March 7.[back]

3. Whitman contributed a series of poems and prose pieces to the New York Herald at the invitation of the editor, James Gordon Bennett, Jr. From December 1887 through August 1888, 33 of Whitman's poems were published in the paper.[back]

4. The poem "After the Dazzle of Day" was published in the New York Herald on February 3, 1888.[back]

5. The poem "Halcyon Days" was published in the Herald on January 29, 1888.[back]

6. The poem "To Those Who've Fail'd" was published in the Herald on January 27, 1888.[back]

7. The poem "America" was published in the Herald on February 11, 1888.[back]

8. The poem "Abraham Lincoln, Born Feb. 12, 1809" was published in the Herald on February 12, 1888.[back]

9. The poem "Soon Shall Winter's Foil Be Here" was published in the Herald on February 21, 1888.[back]

10. The poem "The Dismantled Ship" was published in the Herald on February 23, 1888.[back]
