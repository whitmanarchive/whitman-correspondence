I should suffer much if it was not for Ellen Ellen


Friday Nov 13 1



My dear Brother

I have & still suffer so much agony I am like a child I have longed so for a letter I have seen one to Charles with a few lines to me If I could see one of my own [ folks?] I see in that letter that you wrote of comeing I suppose dear brother that is asking too much. I dont suffer now, only my hand, the fever is gone my head is clear my mind has not wandered only when I [ dozed?] I sometimes think you have written. I have been [ truharted ?] or refused so many things I did not like to tell mother but first Charlie was very ugly he would not get a nurse. dear brother I find I am to weak to write. Dr Thayer I believe thinks all my thumb wont get well I feel very [ anxious?] about it. dear brother write to Dr [ Samuel?] B Thayer. & thank him first Charlie refused to let me go for him. Saturday Nov 14. Dear brother I suffered dreadfully last night with my hand the Doctor did not come in yesterday I was [ greatly?] frightened about my hand & Charlie scolded me till I thought I should be thrown in a fever again I make very much trouble but I suffer too terribly to be scolded. I think I shall get well if I dont get fever but will suffer much my hand is much more painful you know I cant even be roused in bed on account of raising my hand its hard to write Charlie last night brought me your letter to him I read it many times its strange he did not tell you how I asked so much for letter, you speak dear brother of knowing constantly about my illness. I know that Charlie wrote many times [ mornings?] without comeing in to see what kind of a night I had passed my strength fails. I cant say much but Charlie was kind to [ about?] about 2 or 3 days then after doctor scolded him if I am to be sick much longer I do so want one of my own that belongs to me &

be pleasant to Charlie while I am

sick on my account

Ellen is very good to me

feels interested if I only could I dont suppose Mother being sick & our Mother could come and I am afraid it would be too hard for her I will ask [ fr?] how long before I can get just a little relief from pain if he ever comes Charlie has gone for him I want you to write to him I have often spoken of you let [illegible] brother of all things in I beg you to not let Charlie know I have wrote to you. I run a great risk risk . Write to me, at once perhaps [ Me?] be better soon

my love

Han

He just been & has lanced my arm he says that is the trouble so perhaps Ill be better

Correspondent:
Hannah Louisa (Whitman) Heyde (1823–1908), youngest sister of Walt Whitman, married Charles Louis Heyde (1822–1890), a French-born landscape painter. Charles Heyde was infamous among the Whitmans for his offensive letters and poor treatment of Hannah. Hannah and Charles Heyde lived in Burlington, Vermont. For more, see Paula K. Garrett, "Whitman (Heyde), Hannah Louisa (d. 1908)," Walt Whitman: An Encyclopedia, ed. J.R. LeMaster and Donald D. Kummings (New York: Garland Publishing, 1998).
