Finley Hospittal
Aug 30th/64

Washington, D. C.



Friend Walt

I received your letter today & was verry glad to hear from you wonse more I have bin wounded again but it has got well it hit me in the right thigh this time I have had A pretty hard time of it this summer A marching & fighting together

dear walt I am so glad to hear from you once more I would like to see you verry much I have drempt of you often & thought of you oftener still

I expect to leave here tomorrow morning for Carlisle barraks I was to gow this morning but did not & now I am to gow tomorrow morning I guess that they wont put it off anny longer my time is out in eleven days now I have bin home on A furlow I shant have to do anny more fighting I think

Write soon no more at present thew

Smith

Address

Bethuel Smith
Co. F. US. Cav.
Carlisle, Barracks. Penn

Correspondent:
Christopher and Maria Smith were the parents of Bethuel Smith, Company F, Second U.S. Cavalry, who was wounded in 1863 and met Whitman shortly after. He wrote to Whitman on , from the U.S. General Hospital at Carlisle, Pennsylvania, "I left the armory hospital in somewhat of A hurry." He expected, he explained on , to rejoin his regiment shortly, and was stationed near Washington when he wrote on . He wrote on , from Culpeper, Virginia, that he was doing provost duty, and on , he was in a camp near Mitchell Station, Virginia, where "the duty is verry hard." He was wounded again on June 11, 1864 (so his parents reported to Whitman on ), was transported to Washington, and went home on furlough on July 1. He returned on August 14 to Finley Hospital, where, on , he wrote to Whitman: "I would like to see you verry much, I have drempt of you often & thought of you oftener still." He expected to leave the next day for Carlisle Barracks to be mustered out, and on , he wrote to Whitman from Queensbury, N.Y. When his parents communicated with Walt Whitman on , Bethuel was well enough to perform tasks on the farm. Smith was one of the soldiers to whom Whitman wrote ten years later; see Walt Whitman to Bethuel Smith, December 1874 (Edwin Haviland Miller, ed., The Correspondence [New York: New York University Press, 1961–77], 2:318–319).
