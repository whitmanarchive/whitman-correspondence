Oct 22 1864



Dear friend walt

I arrived home the 18 I had to stop over my [ view?] on account of sheridan fighting in the valley mother told me that you wanted me to stop & see you when I come home but it is to late now & I did not hear of it in time there is of considerable excitement here about the election.

my health is not verry good at present I have got diarrhea some I think that it is on account of the change of climate & of food that makes me so although I feel verry well

no more at present

good by From

B Smith

to Friend Walt



Address
Bethuel Smith.
GLens Falls.
Warren. C.O.
N.Y.

Correspondent:
Bethuel Smith, Company F, Second U.S. Cavalry, was wounded in 1863. He wrote to Whitman on , from the U.S. General Hospital at Carlisle, Pennsylanvia, "I left the armory hospital in somewhat of A hurry." He expected, he explained on , to rejoin his regiment shortly, and was stationed near Washington when he wrote on . He wrote on , from Culpeper, Virginia, that he was doing provost duty, and on , he was in a camp near Mitchell Station, Virginia, where "the duty is verry hard." He was wounded again on June 11 (so his parents reported to Whitman on ), was transported to Washington, and went home on furlough on July 1. He returned on August 14 to Finley Hospital, where, on , he wrote to Whitman: "I would like to see you verry much, I have drempt of you often & thought of you oftener still." When his parents communicated with Walt Whitman on , Bethuel was well enough to perform tasks on the farm. Smith was one of the soldiers to whom Whitman wrote ten years later; see Whitman's letter to Bethuel Smith, December 1874 (Edwin Haviland Miller, ed., The Correspondence, 6 vols. [New York: New York University Press, 1961–77], 2:318–319).
