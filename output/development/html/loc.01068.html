OFFICE OF THE

NEW YORK CITIZEN

32 Beekman Street, N. Y.

TERMS OF THE PAPER,

$5.00 PER YEAR

10 CENTS PER COPY.

NEW YORK,
Nov 18 1867



Walt Whitman.

Dear Sir:

Do you remember that I wrote to you, asking if you could not give us a poem, and if so, on what terms?

May I call your attention once more to the matter. We should be very glad to have a poem from you, but I don't know how much the proprietors would authorize me to pay.

When you are in town if you will call on me, I can show you two ladies—one of them my wife—who appreciate your poetry, as I do.

It is only of late years that I have understood it; & I am now enthusiastic over it.

Very Respectfully Yours

W. L. Alden.

(Associate Editor)

Correspondent:
William Livingston Alden (1837–1908) was associate editor of the New York Citizen.
