Camden, N.J.
September 26.1



Dear Sir,2

I am delighted to please you in so trifling a matter as signing the pictures for your—and my—English friends—(substituting portraits I like better, instead of those you sent, which I don't like—though they are fine bits of work.) . . .



Walt Whitman

Notes:

1. Transcript.[back]

2. According to the New York Directory of 1874–1875, Gillette, which Whitman spelled Gilette (see Whitman's letter to Gillette), was a clerk in the county courthouse. An undated entry in one of Walt Whitman's address books (Thomas Biggs Harned Collection of Walt Whitman, The Library of Congress, Notebook #108) indicates that Gillette was at one time employed in the postmaster's office in New York.[back]
