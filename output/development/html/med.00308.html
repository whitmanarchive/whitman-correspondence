Washington,
March 13, 1865



My dear Sir:

Would you do me the favor, if convenient, immediately on receiving this, of enclosing the sketch by Capt. Sims,1 and directing it, Capt. George W. Whitman, Portland Av. near Myrtle, Brooklyn New York—

How are you getting along in New York and how is your brother, Major Wright?2—I send him best regards. I am still here in Washington, and nothing new.

Yours, etc.



Walt Whitman

Correspondent:
David F. Wright wrote to Walt Whitman on , relating his attempts to arrange for the exchange of George, who was in a Confederate prison at that time; see Whitman's letter to William D. O'Connor of .

In Whitman scholarship, this letter traditionally has been identified as "to Dana F. Wright," based on Wright's signature at the end of his letter to Whitman. Descendants of Wright as well as military roster and census data confirm Wright's first name as David.
