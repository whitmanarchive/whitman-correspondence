New Britain
July 24th 1875.



Dear Friend and Father

I received your letter last Eve and was glad to hear from you but I am sory to hear that you have sick so long I have been sick with the Chills & Fever I have had [ wine?] to day my wife and Child have gone home on a visit to her house at Norwalk Conn have been gone cinse the 20th of June so I am alone I am at the same employment yet I have plenty of work but the pay is small My boy was not very well before he went away he has been better cine they have been there they are near the Salt water my wife is well [illegible] the young Lady you met with me at the Cor of Fulton and Cart Sts that is my wife. Last wensday I went to Hartford to the reunion of the Conn Vetrans and spent two days I met a good many of my old camrads we had a good time there wa asembled about Four thousand at charter Oak Park near Hartford. I have grown thin cince you last saw me I do not weigh so much in [illegible] 125 lbs I have worked very hard this sumer and I do not think it agrees so well with me hered as it does near the salt water and I shall not stay here any longer than I can help so good by for this time

I remain yours &c

John M. Rogers

Correspondent:
John (Jack) M. Rogers was a Brooklyn driver with whom Whitman presumably had a loving relationship. Whitman first met him in Brooklyn on September 21, 1870. For more on this, see Charley Shively (ed) Calamus Lovers. Walt Whitman's Working Class Camerados (San Francisco: Gay Sunshine Press, 1987, pp. 122-135).
