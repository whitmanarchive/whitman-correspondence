Washington,
26 November, 1866.



My dear Whitman,

I recd this morning from an old friend (Mr. Robert Buchanan)1 in England a letter and among other things he asks me to send him "Leaves of Grass, Drum Taps and any other works by the author Walt Whitman of the Attorney General's Department, Washington, and a portrait if it is to be got by hook or crook. I shall make a copy from each negative. Please tell me if there are any other works of yours than the above & where I can get them. He desires also that I should send him a portrait of Emerson—Can you tell me where I can get a good one

Yours respectfully,

Alex. Gardner.

Correspondent:
Alexander Gardner (1821–1882) was a Scottish-born American photographer, well-known for his depictions of the Civil War.

Notes:

1. Robert Buchanan (1841–1901), a Scottish poet, novelist and dramatist, was an ardent supporter of Walt Whitman's works in England (see Harold Blodgett, "Whitman and Buchanan," American Literature, 2:2 [May 1930], 131-40).[back]
