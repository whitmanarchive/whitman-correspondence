328 Mickle Street
Camden New Jersey1
June 14 '87



Dear S E C

I wish to send you my special deep-felt personal thanks for your kindness & generosity to me—



Walt Whitman

Correspondent:
Samuel Clemens (1835–1910), better know by his pen name, Mark Twain, had attended Whitman's New York lecture in April. He also contributed to Thomas Donaldson's fund for the purchase of a horse and buggy for Whitman (see Whitman's letter [note 4]), as well as to the fund to build Whitman a private cottage (see Whitman's letter to Sylvester Baxter). Twain was reported in the Boston Herald of May 24, 1887 to have said: "What we want to do is to make the splendid old soul comfortable" (Clara Barrus, Whitman and Burroughs: Comrades [1931], 268).
