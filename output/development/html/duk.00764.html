Howard Lake
July 1515 1874



Mr Walt Whitman

Dear Friend It is with Pleasure I take the opportunity of writing you a letter As my Husband became Acquainted with you In Washington. And He Has took So much time And pleasure In telling me of you. And How kind you were to Him that you Seem to me Almost Like a brother. And I Have often thought of writing to you. William Received Some papers from you yesterday Which we All take great pleasure In Reading And, [ Aspearaly ?] those Poems you Have written, they Are Splendid. ther is a Preaspeterian Minister[ Trevening ?] with my Sister who takes great delight In Reading those Papers

His Home Is In Philadelphia His Name is David Myrdock

Well you Said you would Like to visit Howard Lake. I wished you would for I do believe you would get your Health Hear We would be Happy to Have you visit us, But we are Poor.

We have found out what the Privatians of life is Since we came west we Came Here For williams Health He was very Healthy Before he went to the Army. they Sent him home From Washington withe the fever He Meet his Brother In one mild of Home He Passed By him as He would a Stranger He did Not know Him He Has Never Received Nothing From the government. I think He Aught to Have a pentian .. I thought I would Ask your Advice And Asistence In trying For A pentian .. I will Send you His Discharge you will Please give me your Advice. tell me if you think He can get a pentian And How to get it.......... & We are expecting a letter From you their Has been two Letters written to you Since we Received Any Answer one was sent the first of June & one Since I see In the papers you Sent Reports From Camden of the 4 dat of July their was a Sabath School Pipnic Here My Mother & Father wor there My Mother Is very old I don't Suppose She will Be with us on the [illegible] of July In the comeing year

Well I hope you will get your Health Better, you Will Please Excuse this

My Best Wishes to you

From your Comrades Wife

Jane Stansberry

Please write Soon

Jane Stansberry

Howard Lake

Wright Co. Minn

Please write Soon

Correspondent:
Jane Stansberry was the wife of William Stansberry, a former soldier with whom Whitman had met in Armory Square Hospital.
