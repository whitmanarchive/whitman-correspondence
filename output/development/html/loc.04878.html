1833 Spruce Street

Philadelphia.1



My dear Mr. Whitman

I am asking some friends to take a cup of tea tomorrow (Tuesday afternoon) between four and six o'clock, with miss Gale, the bright young actress who is supporting Booth and Barrett at the Chestnut St. Opera house this week.

Would it be possible for you to come. I know she would

enjoy so much the meeting you and seeing you.

If you cannot come as I fear you cannot, she would prize very much a few words of regret from your own hand. If you will send it [illegible] here, I will see that she gets it.

Miss Gale is one of our most, if not our most promising young actors—

Wishing we might have the pleasure of seeing you.

Very sincerely

Sophia Wells Royce Williams

Correspondent:
Sophia Wells Royce Williams (1850–1928) was a writer and frequent visitor (with her husband Talcott Williams) to Whitman's Camden, New Jersey, home.
