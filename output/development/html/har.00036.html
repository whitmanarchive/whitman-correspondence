Attorney General's Office,

Washington.
March 15, 1870.



Andrew & Thom. Rome,

Dear friends,

I suppose the MS. of "Passage to India" came safe, last week—to be put in type as requested—with p. o. order for $10—I write now merely to ask you to read the proof carefully by copy—correct carefully—& then send me two proofs—put it through as soon as convenient.



Walt.

I have a piece in next Galaxy1—about three or four sticks full—about Spring—I wish you would just copy it in your paper & send me 10 copies—

Correspondent:
The Romes were old Brooklyn friends. In their printing establishment Walt Whitman set up the first edition of Leaves of Grass.

Notes:

1. "A Warble for Lilac-Time" appeared in the Galaxy, 9 (May 1870), 686. For images of the poem as it appeared in the Galaxy, see "Warble for Lilac-Time." [back]
