Sept. the 17, 1863

Carlisle barracks

Friend Walt I take my pen in hand to let you now where I am I am at Carlisle barracks in the hospittal I am getting along verry well now we stop in tents here & it looks verry loansome here to me

Write soon Direct your letters to Bethuel Smith, Carlisle Barracks, Pennsylvania

Correspondent:
Bethuel Smith, Company F, Second U.S. Cavalry, was wounded in 1863. He wrote to Whitman on , from the U.S. General Hospital at Carlisle, Pennsylanvia, "I left the armory hospital in somewhat of A hurry." He expected, he explained on , to rejoin his regiment shortly, and was stationed near Washington when he wrote on . He wrote on , from Culpeper, Virginia, that he was doing provost duty, and on , he was in a camp near Mitchell Station, Virginia, where "the duty is verry hard." He was wounded again on June 11 (so his parents reported to Whitman on ), was transported to Washington, and went home on furlough on July 1. He returned on August 14 to Finley Hospital, where, on , he wrote to Whitman: "I would like to see you verry much, I have drempt of you often & thought of you oftener still." He expected to leave the next day for Carlisle Barracks to be mustered out, and on , he wrote to Whitman from Queensbury, New York. When his parents communicated with Walt Whitman on , Bethuel was well enough to perform tasks on the farm. Smith was one of the soldiers to whom Whitman wrote ten years later; see Whitman's letter to Bethuel Smith, December 1874 (Edwin Haviland Miller, ed., The Correspondence, 6 vols. [New York: New York University Press, 1961–77], 2:318–319).
