January 14, 1870



Dear Walt Whitman

I received your vary kind Letter and picture and am vary glad to hear from you. I all so Received your vary kind Papers, and have [ taken?] great pains to lay them away to look at some future time, but the book I have never seen.

Well now I will tell you about my mother. She sits in her chair kniting a pair of mitens . She says that she has a great angsity to See you my father would like to see you also. My brother [ is enquiring?] I we could all meet you with a harty welcome.

Mother has a very bad cold at praisent , the rest of us are usaly well.

I will send you one dollar to get some pictures with the picture you sent me of your self I take a good look at it every now and then. I thank you vary much for the last one you sent, the views that you sent me are to be of the public buildings of the city. I expect to start for Kansas next Tuesday but my [illegible] had to postpone it for a few days on the [ account?] of death in the family

If you write soon I shall get it

I expect tonight some friend here to make an evening call. You will pleas excuse me from writing as I wish to have some room for my father as he said he would finish this. So fare the my Dear Walt do not forget to address Williamson, Wayne Co. NY

[ Much?] [ respected?] [illegible]

Friend W Whitman it is with the greatest pleasure I attempt to write a few lines to fill out Alfred's letter we all feel a good the warmest affection for you for you very great [ poems have?] conferred on us in writing to us when our son Alfred was sick among strangers and among strangers we have formed a great desire to see you and have a good and friendly talk. We did think that you would start out in the bad weather and get out of the city and see us but now my boy is a going to Kansis to live he expects to start for Kansas with some of our neighbours next Tuesday a week to settle perhaps you may think it not whorth a while to come and see us but we are very anxious to see you if you think you can posibly come have [illegible] here of 130 acres 3 [ horses?] [illegible] two year old colts 9 cows and expect 4 more in the [ spring?] (that is 4 heifers come in [illegible] on the [ next?] steer) (continued for want of room) and a lot of poltry such as turkeys Ducks and chickens

Oh I had a daughter last aug a year ago and she lives with us yet but I expect to be able [ leave?] with her in a few days she ways about 100 lbs and is as handsome as a picture I think I will have her picture before she goes and I will send you one

please except our thanks for past favours.

Mrs Pratt send you her respects in particular

Please write soon to your unworthy friends



John B. Pratt

P.O. Williamson[ Wayne?] Co,NY





Walt Whitman

Dear Friend

I am now at [ my?] own home but have not got my discharge yet. I left Washington on trans furr to rochester .

I have to go back to rochester to get my discharge.

the day that I left thare avent to the [ patent office?] to see you but you was not thare , and I had not time to go whare you was but, avent back to ward C and gave to [ no C?] thare in the ward 70 cts to give to you he said that he would let you have it the first [ time?] that you came in. Now pleas rite and let me know if you got it I wanted to see you I would like to no if you got that money anyhow. I got [illegible] two weeks ago last [ Friday?] , but I find myself rather weak yet last friday I was on A [ lode ?] of hay and went to get down of from it and I fell and scard the [ horses?] one them kicked me on the forehead then they start to run and the wagon wheel struck me on the back of my head and [ hurt?] me bad but can sit rite now. I think of [ no more?] at preasent. Pleas rite [ if?] you get this

Address

Alfred E Pratt

Aug the 7th WilliamsonWayne Co.N Y

My friends [ their?] respects Pleas if get this rite and if you will [ Pleas send?] me that photograph of abe and Washington like yours if you can find them and I will Say I glad for it
