Furze Down, Streatham

Surrey, S. W.
July. 16. 71.



Dear Mr. Whitman.

Tennyson writes to you by this mail he lays upon me the blame of not having written to you sooner, & I am willing to bear it the fact is the books went to his London address & were not forwarded.

Yours affectny ,

Cyril Flower

Correspondent:
Cyril Flower (1843–1907) was an English barrister and a friend of Tennyson; see Harold Blodgett, Walt Whitman in England (1934), 128–129. According to the February 20, 1886 Solicitor's Journal, Flower was appointed a Lord of the Treasury; Flower served as a member of Parliament from 1880 to 1892, when he was given the title Baron Battersea (see the London Gazette (6 September 1892), 5090). According to Flower's letter, he met Whitman in Washington in December, 1870. He had later delivered some of Whitman's books to Tennyson, who "was much touched by your memory of him, and I told him of your deep regard for him." On , Flower informed Whitman that Tennyson was sending a letter by the same mail (Tennyson's letter was dated ). Flower wrote again on : "When I read you or think of you . . . I feel that I hold in my hand clasped strong & tight & for security the great hand of a friend, a simple good fellow, a man who loves me & who is beautiful because he loves, & with the consciousness of that I feel never alone—never sad."
