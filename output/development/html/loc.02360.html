Telford Bucks Co Pa1
9.12.1888



Walt Whitman

Dear Friend,

I send today by Express a basket of fruit it ought to be emptied right away. The golden rod on the top will make a boquet for you, let me know if the 2 bottles of wine got broke I hope you are feeling better Mrs Ingram still keeps weak but is able to be around I am kept very busy looking after the fruit we all send much love

from Your Friend

Wm Ingram

Correspondent:
William Ingram, a Quaker, kept a tea store—William Ingram and Son Tea Dealers–in Philadelphia. Of Ingram, Whitman observed to Horace Traubel: "He is a man of the Thomas Paine stripe—full of benevolent impulses, of radicalism, of the desire to alleviate the sufferings of the world—especially the sufferings of prisoners in jails, who are his protégés" (Horace Traubel, With Walt Whitman in Camden, Sunday, May 20, 1888).
