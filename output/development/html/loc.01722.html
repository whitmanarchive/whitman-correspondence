Printing, Stereotyping, and Binding of Every Description, Promptly and Well Done at the Lowest Rates for Cash.

S.W. GREEN, successor to

JOHN A. GRAY & GREEN,

Nos. 16 and 18 [ S?] COB STREET, NEW-YORK.
Aug 9.th 1872



Walt Whitman,

Dear sir,

Your favor of 8th inst containing ($50#) Fifty Dollars was received in due course from the Adams Express Co. I have placed the same to credit.

w/c—

Yours Resply

S. W. Green

Correspondent:
Samuel W. Green was listed in Goulding's New York City Directory (1877–1878) as a printer at 18 Jacob, h 123 Livingston, Brooklyn. Among his skills and services, Green listed a "book and job department," a "stereotype and electrotype foundry," "bookbindery," and "printing of every description."
