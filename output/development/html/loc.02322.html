208 West 24th, St NY.
Dec. 16th. 1888



Walt Whitman Esq

Dear Sir.

Since I sent you a proof some time ago I have worked up the etching and I think much improved it. I now forward you another proof—do you like it, or is there anything you think would improve it—if you approve of it and will let me know I shall be most happy to send you the proofs I promised—would you like 6 Artists proofs of Walt Whitman, or any of the enclosed list

very truly yours

Samuel Hollyer

Correspondent:
Samuel Hollyer (1826–1919), an etcher and engraver, emigrated to the United States in 1851. 3His engraving of Whitman as a laborer appears in the first edition of Leaves of Grass. Whitman sent Hollyer the photograph called "Lear" (reproduced in The Correspondence, ed. Edwin Haviland Miller, vol. 4, following page 278, and in Specimen Days [1971], plate 180). Whitman referred favorably to the finished etching on , in his letter to Richard Maurice Bucke, and in his Commonplace Book on the preceding day (see Charles E. Feinberg Collection of the Papers of Walt Whitman, 1839–1919, Library of Congress, Washington, D.C.). For Whitman's reservations later, see Horace Traubel, With Walt Whitman in Camden, Sunday, August 12, 1888 and Wednesday, August 15, 1888.
