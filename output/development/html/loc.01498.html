431 Stevens street
Camden New Jersey
US America
Sept 8 '761



Edward Dowden

My dear friend

I send you by mail to-day, same address as this note, 1 copy, Leaves of Grass and two copies Two Rivulets—Also the set, Two Vols. for Rev. A B Grosart2 as the address sent me for Mr. G's books dont appear to me specific & full enough. I have also sent same mail with this a set, Two Vols. directed Rev. Prof. Dowden, North Brantisfield Pl. Edinburgh, Scotland—(Is that address right?—The Scotland was not on the address as sent me)—I have also sent a copy of Two Rivulets to Rev. R P Graves, 1 Watson r'd , Leeson Park Dublin. (Eight Volumes altogether, for which the pay was some time since rec'd by me from Wm Rossetti,) Please send me word soon as they reach you safely—also whether Rev. Mr. Dowden's set was directed right—My second batch of the new edition (600 copies each Vol.) is at last now ready, & I promptly supply orders henceforward. I have many things to say to you my dear friend.

I am sitting here by the open window, writing this, 'most sundown—feeling pretty well for me, as things go—often think of you & yours, & the friends in Ireland—must close as I want to send this to catch to-morrow's New York mail.3



Walt Whitman.

Notes:

1. This letter is addressed: Prof. Edw: Dowden | Temple Rd Winstead | Rathmines | Dublin | Ireland. It is postmarked: Camden | Sep |8 | N.J.; Philadelphia, P, A | Sep | [illegible].[back]

2. Grossart (1827–1899) was a clergyman and also the indefatigable editor of Elizabethan texts.[back]

3. On Dowden acknowledged receipt of the volumes, and the impending publication of a volume of his poems (The Charles E. Feinberg Collection of the Papers of Walt Whitman, 1839–1919, Library of Congress, Washington, D.C.). See also Horace Traubel, With Walt Whitman in Camden, Saturday, August 4, 1888.[back]
