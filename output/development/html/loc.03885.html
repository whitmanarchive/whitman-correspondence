Sunday Feb 15th 91



Dear Mr Whitman

You have looked for a letter from me long be for this I have had so much sickness in the family this winter that I have been kept busy. Ed1 has been real sick the past two weeks he caught a heavy cold it settled on the stomach & bowls I was afraid inflamation would set in but he has got better & is now able to work some. he has gone down to Mont[']s2 to day . Pap is much better I think by spring he will be as well as usual. The money you sent by Herbert3 I distributed as you directed the remaining 8 dollars

I gave to pap as he has a hundred 100. dollars to make up by the 25th & we have had so much bad luck this year he does not know just where the money is to come from Aunt Hannah4 is still with me her Daughter5 pays 2 dollars per week for her board while she is here. I got a letter from Ruth6 last night they seem to be geting on out there & they like the place & people.

I thought I would like to go home I mean to my Brother[']s7 at Bridgeport the 20th of this month he has asked me to come. I don't know whether I will be able to get off or not I would like to go & have a few days rest if I can get some good body to keep house for me while all seem to be well Harry8 & Eva9 were at home yesterday

they were well Harry has not got any thing to do yet he has began to think the Rail Road company is not going to give him a thing to do. I hope they will he is so anxious to get to work Joe & Debbie10 are well George11 was at home this week & Van12 is at home to day write to us please when you are well enough how much we all would like to see you with us once more

with much love

S M Stafford

Correspondent:
Susan M. Stafford was the mother of Harry Stafford, who, in 1876, became a close friend of Whitman while working at the printing office of the Camden New Republic. Whitman regularly visited the Staffords at their family farm near Kirkwood, New Jersey. Whitman enjoyed the atmosphere and tranquility that the farm provided and would often stay for weeks at a time (see David G. Miller, "Stafford, George and Susan M.," Walt Whitman: An Encyclopedia, ed. J.R. LeMaster and Donald D. Kummings [New York: Garland Publishing, 1998], 685).
