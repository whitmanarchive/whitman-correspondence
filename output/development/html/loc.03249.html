1524 Walnut Street

Philadelphia



My Dear Good Gray Poet—

Ever since I bought the first edition of Leaves of Grass we have been friends through your books—I warmly thank you for this precious memorial of a man whose life work + example are better even than his books

and I am

gratefully

yr. friend

Weir Mitchell Walt. Whitman Dec 15, 1889

Correspondent:
Dr. S. Weir Mitchell (1829–1914) was a specialist in nervous disorders as well as a poet and a novelist. On April 18, Whitman had his second interview with Dr. Mitchell, who attributed his earlier paralysis to a small rupture of a blood vessel in the brain but termed Whitman's heart "normal and healthy." Whitman also noted that "the bad spells [Mitchell] tho't recurrences by habit (? sort of automatic)" (Whitman's Commonplace Book, Charles E. Feinberg Collection of the Papers of Walt Whitman, 1839–1919, Library of Congress, Washington, D.C.). Mitchell was the first physician to indicate the psychosomatic nature of many of Whitman's ailments. Probably the impending lecture on the death of Lincoln unconsciously brought back the emotional involvements of his hospital experiences with comrades whom he had come to love only to be separated from them.
