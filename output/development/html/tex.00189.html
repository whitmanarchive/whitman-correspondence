Attorney General's Office

Washington,
June 4, 1869



Dear Sir:1

Your letters have been received. I cordially accept your appreciation & sympathy.

I send you, same mail with this, a copy of my book, as requestedâ€”Also a copy of Mr. Burroughs's "Notes," which I have procured. Also a late photograph, accompanied by a picture taken in 1855, very good at the time. Also a late newspaper.

Since the book, there have been several pieces of mine, in magazines.2 I will endeavor to procure them, (or some of them,) & forward you.

I send you my thanks & good will.

Walt Whitman
