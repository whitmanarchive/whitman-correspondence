THE PICTORIAL WORLD,

Illustrated Newspaper,

OFFICES: 63, FLEET STREET, LONDON.
May 31st 1875.



Walt Whitman Esq.

Dear Sir,

As one of your admirers, and as this comes of Mr. Robert Buchanan, another of your admirers, I want you to do me a favor—that is, to write me a lyric—something in the style of that on Abraham Lincoln—for a new monthly magazine I am about to bring out. Will you do this?

As a further clue to my identity, I may tell you that I am editor of this paper and English correspondent of Appleton's Journal.

Very faithfully yours,

Will Williams.

P.S. The magazine in question will contain contributions of well-known English and American authors.

Correspondent:
As yet we have no information about this correspondent.
