April 26 1870



My Dear friend Walt

I now take my pen in hand to let you know how I am getting along [ .?] I am in very good health at present and I hope you are the same. my father is not very well at present he has been Laid up for a week with the Rhumatism [ .?] In his feet he is getting Better. I am very sorry to have any bad news to tell you but I can not help it. I have got in trouble I bought some potent [ medicine?] to the amount of three Dollars. I bought some medicine [ for?] a friend of my fathers & he gave me the money to pay for them & I Spent it I am very sorry I Done it. I have Learnt a lesson from it witch will last me a life time. We have a new [ basher's?] & he is looking over the books & he seen the account against me & he says if I Do not pay it before Saturday night he will tell Robbins & then I will be Disgraced & Discarged I asked my Sister to lend three Dollars [ &?] she would not. She said I might get Discrased for all She cared & now I apply to you the last friend I have in this world I wish you would lend three Dollars & I will pay you as soon as I can earn it you will oblidge me very much & save me from being Disgraced. this is all at present, I will write as soon as I hear from you

and now good by from

your friend with love

Walt Whitman [ R?] 1

I will put this in an [ &?] try Envelope so that if you Do not Receive it I will get it without any one seeing it.

yours &c. WR

Correspondent:
Mrs. Henry Reynolds, mother of Walter Whitman Reynolds, wrote to Walt Whitman on , imploring Whitman to get to know his namesake, who "is a nice boy, between 13 and 14 years old. and i thought perhaps you might take an interest in him." Walter Whitman Reynolds also wrote to Whitman on , , and . In this last letter, Reynolds begged Whitman, "friend Walt I want to know if you will be kind enough loan me twenty dollars as I want to get a pair of pants and a coat." If Whitman replied, his letters are not extant. According to Whitman's daybook, his namesake Reynolds visited him on September 1, 1889.
