Washington
Oct the 13 1863



Dear Friend

I now sit down to write to write you a few lines to let you now where I am I am with the regiment now. I left Carlisle about one week ago and have ben diledaleying around & have not had time to rite to you untill now & I have not got much time now the toe is most all healed up but my foot is swelled so that I can not get my boot on it swelled from walking from the depot [ out?] her but I think that will go down in a day or to the brigade is gone out to the front again & I dont now but we shall follow them pretty soon I dont now as I can write anny more at present So good by for this time



Bethuel Smith


Co. F. 2nd U. S. Cav.

Washington DC

Correspondent:
Bethuel Smith, Company F, Second U.S. Cavalry, was wounded in 1863. He wrote to Whitman on , from the U.S. General Hospital at Carlisle, Pennsylanvia, "I left the armory hospital in somewhat of A hurry." He wrote on , from Culpeper, Virginia, that he was doing provost duty, and on , he was in a camp near Mitchell Station, Virginia, where "the duty is verry hard." He was wounded again on June 11 (so his parents reported to Whitman on ), was transported to Washington, and went home on furlough on July 1. He returned on August 14 to Finley Hospital, where, on , he wrote to Whitman: "I would like to see you verry much, I have drempt of you often & thought of you oftener still." He expected to leave the next day for Carlisle Barracks to be mustered out, and on , he wrote to Whitman from Queensbury, New York. When his parents communicated with Walt Whitman on , Bethuel was well enough to perform tasks on the farm. Smith was one of the soldiers to whom Whitman wrote ten years later; see Whitman's letter to Bethuel Smith, December 1874 (Edwin Haviland Miller, ed., The Correspondence, 6 vols. [New York: New York University Press, 1961–77], 2:318–319).
