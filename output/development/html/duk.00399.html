Burlington Vermont.
Jany 17, 1885



Bro Walt.

Your card duly recd:1 also Camden paper:2 She3 cannot read either,4 or have them read, or mentioned to her: she will read them when she gets better. She is much stronger, essays make her own bed, and cook a little for herself; what she fancies, relishes, in her own way. Her physical condition improves daily: her infirmity or disorder is in her brain: cannot sleep, [illegible] but fitfully: says she can feel her heart throb in her brain: Cannot endure conversation: wont have me read the paper to her, or mention circumstances of [ any?] character. Grows nervous should a neighbour visit. asks them to excuse her. The weather severe, cold, wild winds raging—I run 3 stoves, night and day; to keep house comfortable: My lottery has not come of—[ dull?] with every body: 60 dollars worth of frames: I have sold some shares: I paid an installment of 25 dollars, on my new [ roof?] : fortunate I had it done; with other much needed repairs. Costing or factoring up with stove, coal, and provisions nearly 200 dollars—I hope to realize something—Doctor says Han will get well: nervousness! what a terrible, uncontrollable disorder.



C. L. H—

You mailed Sunday Press. I am a subscriber for a year past.

Correspondent:
Charles Louis Heyde (1822–1890), a French-born landscape painter, married Hannah Louisa Whitman (1823–1890), Walt Whitman's sister, and they lived in Burlington, Vermont. Charles Heyde was infamous among the Whitmans for his offensive letters and poor treatment of Hannah. For more information about Heyde, see Steven Schroeder, "Heyde, Charles Louis (1822–1892)," Walt Whitman: An Encyclopedia, ed. J.R. LeMaster and Donald D. Kummings (New York: Garland Publishing, 1998).

Notes:

1. Whitman's card appears to be lost.[back]

2. This is likely a reference to one of two articles that appeared in the Camden Post (on March 7th and 8th) describing Whitman's meeting with the English poet Sir Edmund William Gosse (1849–1928).[back]

3. Hannah Louisa (Whitman) Heyde, youngest sister of Walt Whitman. For more, see Paula K. Garrett, "Whitman (Heyde), Hannah Louisa (d. 1908)," Walt Whitman: An Encyclopedia, ed. J.R. LeMaster and Donald D. Kummings (New York: Garland Publishing, 1998).[back]

4. Hannah Heyde was frequently sick, but the exact nature of her ailment in 1885 is unknown.[back]
