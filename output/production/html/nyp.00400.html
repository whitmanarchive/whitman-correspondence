Hampden, Ohio, March 10, 1875 Friend Sir

I am glad to have the pleasur​ pleasure​ of wrighting​ writing​ to you again I receicved​ received​ your welcome letter and papers, and your pictur​ picture​ witch​ which​ I prised​ prized​ very much and knew the old friendly face in a moment loks​ looks​ very naturel​ natural​ in dead​ indeed​ and I would like to spend a few oures​ hours'​ talk with you I think we could enjoy a day if not more I shal​ shall​ never forget your hospitality and friendly gifts in time of need may god help you throu​ through​ this world of trouble

I had bad luck with my soildier​ soldier​ money I had a good home and went in pardnership​ partnership​ with a friend. I thot​ thought​ but proved untrue so that is the way with this world a pencion​ pension​ agent stoped​ stopped​ here last knight​ night​ and I showed him your picture he was glad he knew you in a minit​ minute​ was surprised to think I had your pictur​ picture​ I told him I never expected to see you again. I would not take a goodeel​ good deal​ for the picture I expect to see you sum time​ sometime​ I know you are living. Mother remembers the hand righting​ handwriting​ of a letter that you wrote in the Hospital for me thankful to you for it I will right​ write​ you more if you can read this.

so good buy​ goodbye​ for this time and in love and good wishes to you hope to see you.
