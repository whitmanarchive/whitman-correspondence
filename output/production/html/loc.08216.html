  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 16 Nov 1891

Yours of 12th and 14th enclosing Miss May Johnston's was received and heartily welcomed today. Also the bundle of news papers came duly to hand and I was very glad to get them. The date is cut off the N.Y. Recorder con'g "Howards Letter" I want you or Horace to tell it me. I am glad H. has the tomb matter in hand and have no doubt he will soon straighten it up, seems to me clear that they were trying to impose on you but before they let this, they will find that that is not so easy. All very quiet here—raining all day but remarkably warm for 16th Nov. No frost of any account here yet but I guess we shall soon see it cold now—I am not yet quite over Clare's Ball (13–14 inst) still a little stiff about the back—but the young folk had a big time & enjoyed it.

Best love R M Bucke
