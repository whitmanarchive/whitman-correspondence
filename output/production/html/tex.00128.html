Friend witman

I now take the plesure of fulfilling my promace of writing to yo hoping to find yo en Joying good hlth I am not very well i am worse now than wen i got out of bed i tore my wound acoming home the hole is a bout half an inch square I hante ben noware sence i have been home i have had the docter and he ordered me to bathe in sider soaky i will hef to have my furlow exstended I would have written be fore but i was not able to do it yo must exscuse me—

the crops look much better here than they do in washington I find a grate many that donte know me when i arrived in york my cosin was thare and he brought me to my home it has ben rainy ever since I have been home—

give my love to the boys in ward B I will be back Just as soon as i am able to come

No more at present Good by answer by return mail from a friend and ever shall be
