  Superintendent's Office Asylum 
  for the Insane Ontario London, Ont., 28 April 1890

I have your card of 25th Am not well pleased that you continue to feel so miserable Had hoped that the effects of La Grippe would have passed off before this

I hope that Londoners will buy the 100 copies of big book—there is not much money in the sale but it looks well to see the "Englishers" after it

Be sure and tell me when Lippincotts print the piece you sent them—or send me a copy What you say about Tennyson is the first I have heard of any special utterance of his in re "The Leaves" Is there any way of getting hold of what he and the Boston man said—Any thing really good and strong from Tennyson would be quite a benefit and help to us

Love always R M Bucke
