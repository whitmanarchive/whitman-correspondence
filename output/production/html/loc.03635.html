  Boston, 6th December Walt. Whitman Dear Sir–

I have read with pleasure, your "Leaves of Grass," and admire their impudence and beauty,—but can't say which I admire most. I have lent my copy to others. Some are in ecstasies over it, while others think no man should be allowed to print, write, buy, sell or read so wicked a book.

Now you may call me a fool for my request, but if you will comply with it, I shall be just as well satisfied. I want your autograph attached to an extract from your leaves,   or, to a fresh Leaf. I wish to frame it, to hang in my room, in company with a autograph of Charles Mackay, another of my favorites. Please write me on a half-sheet of note paper,—this size—&return according to the endorsed envelope.

Truly yours, Wm Robinson Wm Robinson (ask'g autograph)
