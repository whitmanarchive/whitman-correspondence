  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 25 May 1891

I do not hear from you. Feel uneasy abt. you all the time. Fear you are having a bad time perhaps very sick. Am strongly minded to go to Camden next friday & see for myself how you are. May drop in upon you Saturday morning and stay around till monday or Tuesday. Am feeling O.K. but still some lame but can get around. Grounds here look well now—lilacs just beginning to come out. We loyal Kanucks are keeping the Queen's birthday—my fam all out fishing—3 parties of them—all off—from my brother 60 yrs old to my youngest 9. Suppose we shall have fish now till we are tired of them! I have spent the day here in office writing and reading—looking thro' "Good-Bye" among other things—I like the little last leaves well—they are just what they should be under all the circumstances not great but very touching & charming

Best love R M Bucke   see notes May 27 1891
