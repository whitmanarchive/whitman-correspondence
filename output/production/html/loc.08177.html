  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 22 Oct 1891

Beemer is away on holidays, Barber sick in bed—Ross and I have to get along best we can—I gave my first lecture Monday, give another Saturday Morning. Three lectures are two hours each so that they each one use up a lot of material. You may judge that I have ^no idle time on my hands. I have your cards of 18th & 20th—thanks! Yes, I wrote Costelloe about a month ago re the Morse head—asked him to have it sent to Dr Johnston, Bolton, unless it was in the hands of some one who cared for it—have not heard from him since. Had a letter from Wallace (dated Camden 20th) this morning—he is a good fellow and a true man and friend—I think very highly indeed of him—

So long! dear Walt Love to you R M Bucke
