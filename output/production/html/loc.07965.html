  Camden N J—U S America Sunday aft: July 26 '91

Quite tolerable wea'r—no oppressive heat—& makes me easier—fair voidance bowel action (wh' is the g't desideratum)—nothing very new—went out by horse & wagon two hours last of yest: afternoon to the north region (Pea shore) edge of water side—very primitive, cheap, &c. Am not writing any thing for print—Suppose you easily get the Lip for Aug. there—It circulates quietly quite extensively, here, the Pacific region & in Australia, & this art: will do my cause g't good. (Horace puts his best f't foremost)—D McKay is out west on a trade jaunt—quite a supper at 5—boil'd clams, onions & potatoes—(a little more & appetite w'd be good)—

July 27 Monday—Fine sunny, not hot—am fairly—some head ache—no word f'm you, yet (only the Bolton cable)—all going well here—am sitting quietly in shirt sleeves by window—

Aff: remembrances to Mr Smith, & to Mary, Alys, Logan & Mr C—to all other friends also— Walt Whitman

This is the 5th I have sent you to Mr Costelloe's care—

  July 26
