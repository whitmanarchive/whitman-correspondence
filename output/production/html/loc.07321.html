  Superintendent's Office. Asylum 
 for the Insane London. Ontario London, Ont., 29 Oct 1889

I have yours of Saturday and Sunday with enclosures—I like the "71st Year" very much—am glad to have Arnold's sonnet and E. Gosse on Tennyson's "Throstle"—I have not seen Ed. Wilkins yet, nor (of course) got the photo's—he is to be here this afternoon or evening I believe. Nothing new from Willy Gurd. I expect to go to Guelph (Ont.) tomorrow to give evidence on a murder trial (whether the homicide is sane or not). I lectured to students 2½ hours yesterday afternoon. We are all well—The poor P. of Wales seems from the reports to be very ill—looks as if he would never ascend the throne

R M Bucke
