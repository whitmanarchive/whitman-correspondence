  Washington, D.C. December 21, 1886. Dear Walt

I enclose the two books by express, and will write you later. You need not return them until called for, if at all. Charley Eldridge sent them to me, I don't know yet whether as a gift or a loan. So take your time at them.

I find on carefully reading the "Quarterly," that I should greatly qualify my first impression of its merits. Still, it is good, and its source   gives it weight, and great significance.

I am badly off today, so excuse crudity and brevity. I hope you'll have a comfortable Christmas.

Affectionately, W D O'Connor

Walt Whitman.
