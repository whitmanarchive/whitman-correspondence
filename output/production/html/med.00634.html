June 9 '79 My dear Josiah Child

I have only just rec'd​ yours of April 22d, with $24.50 from Trübner & Co. for which am't​ see receipt enclosed—

I note the order for nineteen new copies, & as soon as I go back to Camden, (within a week) they shall be carefully forwarded to you. You speak of a remittance for of three guineas, promised to be forwarded by Mr. Fraser for the Tobacco Plant piece. As no such remittance has been rec'd​ by me, nor heard about, it has probably been deferred—Mr. F. can address me, & send P O order to Camden—I shall prepare other two pieces & send him for the paper, in response to his kind request.

Thanks for letter enclosures—Mr. Westness's, Mr Walters's & Mr Norman's. Will try to send you photos for him (Thos:​ Dixon's is not enclosed)—I keep well, for me—have been away from home gallivanting around, land & water, & especially this city, the last two months—I send you a paper with this.

Walt Whitman

(I write this from New York City, U S A. 1309 Fifth av:​ near 86th st.​ : but I return soon to 431 Stevens st:​ Camden New Jersey—which is my permanent P O address—)
