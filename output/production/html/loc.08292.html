6 March [18]91

Yours of 28th ult and 1st inst reached me day before yesterday as I was on my way to Sarnia. I voted there on thursday morning (5th) and at noon returned to London and voted here. The elections are over but we do not yet know the result with any accuracy—the government, however, is no doubt sustained (worse luck) and closer trade relations with you are indefinitely postponed.

Yes, I got the Lips' and like the pieces and the way they came out there much. [—] I hope now not to be so crowded and to have more time to write and keep track of my american affairs—I hope Horace and I shall get at the W. W. book of collect before a great while. I trust you are tolerably comfortable. This I suppose is the most we must hope for untill the spring opens and you get outside again

Best Love R M Bucke
