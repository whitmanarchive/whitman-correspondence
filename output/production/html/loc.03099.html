  TELEGRAPHIC ADDRESS, ℅ LYRISTES, LONDON. LYRIC CLUB, PICCADILLY EAST, W. Dec 14 1891 Walt Whitman Esq

Whilst declicacy of sentiment keeps one back, death may make one deeply regret the vacillation which it will decide, and this [illegible] will live on to regret its want of courage

Will you write on the enclosed sheet of paper a few words that I shall treasure—my cousin Hamilton Aïdé     talked to me of you—& I have for years worshipped in distance reverence, that I cannot doubt that you will do me this little act on your part, bringing such pride & pleasure to me

Your devoted admirer Luther Munday Secretary of the Lyric Club which is the London centre of social & artistic gatherings         Give out the Ingersol telegram tonight. to Jeffrey's & Patterson
