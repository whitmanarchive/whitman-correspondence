  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 29 Oct 1891

Many thanks, dear Walt, for "The Record" (this moment received) containing "Whitman's Nightfall" (daylight is certainly fading but "night" is not yet). When night does come it will be moonlight and full of stars—I am not in any sense terrified of it. All quiet and well here. Meter jogging along hopefully—it has been under a bit of a cloud for a few months but I think I see some clear sky ahead now. I am reading Wigston's book on Bacon which I mentioned to you a few days ago—he points out in a striking manner how B.'s Henry VII dovetails in between S.'s Richard III & S.'s Henry VIII—the same man must have written all three

R M Bucke
