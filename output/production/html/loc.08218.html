  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 10 Nov 1891

I have yours of last Saturday evening and thank you for mentioning the Bacon book by J.E. Roe—I have sent for it—am at present very much interested in this Bacon business. I have sent for the "Three Tales" but have not got them yet. We have had some lovely "Indian summer" weather here but today it has poured down rain since before daylight. It is said that "Annexation" feeling is growing with great rapidity in Canada— I should expect it would the way things are going generally in the country—increasing debt, stationary population, best young men leaving as they grow up. As far as I can judge, dear Walt, you are not going back any these last few months

Best love R M Bucke
