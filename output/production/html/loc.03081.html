  Dear Friend:

Yrs w. enclosures. Tell Traubel that our exch editor clipped notice of the Ingersoll lecture. It came to me in proof (a few lines) & I added that the reference of the lecture wd be to W.W.'s works. It will be a tremendous blow to the anti-Whitmanites—the anti-naturalists, the Christians & ascetics, I feel sure. Glad to see that [illegible] Dr. B. is not the worse for his acci—but stop! I see by the date of yr. enclosed letters Sept. 9 that he was not then thrown out. I do hope the dear good fellow is not shocked in any way by it. Am plodding on; writing a good many little editorial bits of late, am overworked all the time, & don't feel very bright in consequence. But howsoever forge ahead! Am going to see Soudan & Verestchagin again.

W.S.K.

I have an editorial coming on O'Connor's Rock Me to Sleep expose, and another on Incineration.
