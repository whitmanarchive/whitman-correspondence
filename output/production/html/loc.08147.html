  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 8 May 1891

H. & I have been rather in a pucker about you untill this morning's mail brought y'r letters of 4th & 5th and some bundles of papers to H. We are much relieved now to find that you are certainly no worse and may be a little easier. We had a long and most charming drive through the country yesterday—H., Anne, P.E. Bucke, and a couple of girls—today the weather is again perfect—H. will tell you about it, he thinks is worth annexing f'm what he has seen of it so far. We are very busy talking and planning—I guess the W.W. business will be all settled by the end of this week when H. leaves! My oldest boy, Maurice is appointed on the Geological Survey at $60.00, and found—pretty good, eh? Nothing new abt. going to England. The meter goes on all right.

R M Bucke
