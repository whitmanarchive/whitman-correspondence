  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 12 Feb 1891

Thanks, dear Walt, for the paper with the long piece on Koch's lymph. I read it with interest and am glad to see that the American Government is taking a hand in this last medical suggestion which may turn out of some importance. I have a long and very interesting letter from Wallace cheifly​ chiefly​ on the intercommunication of the human and divine. All is well here—we are having bright, quite warm weather—so far we have had an ideal winter. The meter jogs on. We are up to our necks in politics here, please the Lord we will beat old John A. MacDonald this time and get a little more freedom to move

Best Love RM Bucke
