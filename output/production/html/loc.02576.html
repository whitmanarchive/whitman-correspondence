  Telephone Call: 916 21st Street. New York, Sept 23 1890 Dear Walt:

Your postal is rec'd—I sent the material you mailed me to Ingersoll, and have no doubt he will find it useful—

Now then; If you can get to Phil for an even'g—What is to hinder your being wheeled into a car and being whisked over here in two hours, then after three days good solid rest, with Mrs Davis & your nurse to help, you will be able to go to one of our great Halls or Opera house & listen to Ingersoll.

In other words—If the thing comes off here, it will knock Phil. right out—I believe  you can stand it easy—

Alma says "We will give Uncle Walt our floor and he will be as comfortable as any one possibly can be"

We live in Stuyvesant Square, 4 story wide house 3 rooms deep, so you see you can sling cats if you want to.

Think this over, and lets see if we cannot teach the owners of your Academy a lesson.

  16.05 W.H. Neidlinger   Sincerely Yours JH Johnston
