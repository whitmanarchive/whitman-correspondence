Hampden, Ohio, August 8, 1875 Kind sur​ sir​ Uncl​ Uncle​

I recived​ received​ your welcom​ welcome​ and expecd​ expected​ letter was glad to here​ hear​ from you I neglect wrighting​ writing​ for I am not much on a right but I must wright​ write​ to you Moather​ Mother​ ses​ says​ I aught​ ought​ to right​ write​ to one that was a friend in time of need I am glad to know that you are living I should like to see you very much if I could I would but traviling​ traveling​ cost money and I did not get much from where I am I supose​ suppose​ their​ there​ is not much call for help in Campdin​ Camden​ I get 4 dollers​ dollars​ per month pencion​ pension​ but every little helps in hard times very plesent​ pleasant​ weather and crops look well here since the rain we had last weak​ week​ I am working by the month since I came home from York St​

I can come home very​ every​ Sunday and as I looked at your pictur​ picture​ I sed​ said​ I must wright​ write​ eny how​ anyhow​ if ever so poor a letter but you will excuse from a friend though many miles away this world is ful​ full​ of trouble and we and we all have our share sum​ some​ is blest​ blessed​ with health and wealth while others . . . want but I am glad to get along and be most well

I can allways​ always​ find work wright​ write​ again

My love to you and good wishes, Yours truly
