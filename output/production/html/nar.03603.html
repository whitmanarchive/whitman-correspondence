431 Stevens st.  
 Camden,  
 N. Jersey. June 22, 1874.

Would it be convenient to the President to personally request of the Attorney General that in any changes in the Solicitor Treasury's office, I be not disturbed in my position as clerk in that office—all my duties to the government being & having been thoroughly & regularly performed there, by a substitute, during my illness.

I shall probably get well before long.

Very respectfully, Walt Whitman
