Washington, D.C. May 7, 1873. My dear Edmund Yates,

Pardon me for my forgetfulness about the pictures. I send you three, to make it up. I have been putting off every thing—forgetting every thing—till I feel well again—for I am still in a pretty bad way—but shall come round again by-and-by, with the blessing of God—And so, (as Mr. Philp's messenger is waiting) I shake hands across—& abruptly bid you good bye, for this time.

Walt Whitman

My address here is Solicitor's Office, Treasury, and shall always be happy to hear from you.
