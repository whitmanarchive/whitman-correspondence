  Sunday Afternoon Aug 17 1873 Dearest Brother

I am so much afraid you have been worried about me

I hope not—for I care as much about your health as my own

Your letter did me good dear brother you are so kind to speak of coming to see me. I am down stairs, am feeling much better, more so than at any time, I have had such good care. Charlie has been so kind, dear dear brothe​ brother​ thanks for all your letters take good care of yourself   that will do me more good than you think

I am sorry about your lameness. I think about you always.

I shall get well as ever I think in time. How is Eddy
