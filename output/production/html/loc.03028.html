  Belmont June 12 89. Dear W. W.:—

The poem came to hand, (on the Johnston calamity). It adds an important shade of tho't I think to your religious poems yr philosophy. I don't see how you cd do it so quickly. It is firstrate

I did make that condition in my letters to Gardner—i.e. that my corrections on proofs shd be followed. I only surmised that he might be mean enough to go ahead without me. But I have no real ground to think so. Let him take his time.

We are having a rose carnival. Burroughs's curious state of mind is all right. He is happy & moulting. I don't understand him though, do you? I think of Gilder often lately, with some satisfaction.

W. S. K.
