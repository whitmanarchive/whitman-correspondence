  112 M St N. W. August 26. 1891. Dear Walt,

Perhaps Horace Traubel has told you that I am hoping to see you soon.

They kindly asked me to go to Camden to see Dr. Bucke, as I shall not now be able to make the long promised visit to Canada, as I had thought of doing.

It is a great pleasure to contemplate, that of seeing you again. I hope that you are holding your own this summer, & more than that.

You may know that I am expecting to leave Washington, & give up my home in the place that is dearest to me of any on earth, but so   it is, & I shall tell you about it when I see you.

Till then & always—love from your old friend Nelly O'Connor.
