  26 Nelson Square Bolton. England. Feb 24th 1892 Dear Mr Whitman,

Your friend Dr J Johnson​ Johnston​ of this town has today kindly shown me your letter recieved​ received​ by him last week. As a young Newspaper Editor, & as one who values your writings more than I can ever attempt to express, please   allow me to express my kindest & tenderest sympathies with you in your hours of weakness & suffering. Your teachings rest always in my mind like gleams of sunlight upon the pathway of the future, & I may say that I never write a leading article without trying, as much as lies within me, to hold your "Democratic Vistas" in my mind's eye.

In conclusion let me subscribe myself

Yours ever gratefully Max A Wright.
