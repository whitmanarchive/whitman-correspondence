  Superintendent's Office Asylum 
 for the Insane Ontario London, Ont., 4 Feb 1890

A Mr Duffield, Manager of City Gas Co. here, has been out all forenoon with us (Gurd & I) examining the meter (gas m.). He talks of putting in money and assisting us to get it started. His verdict on the gas m. boiled down amounted to this: "Our m. is as good in all respects (better in some) as the m.s at present in use and can be made for about ¼ the cost." If this verdict stands the m. is worth millions of dollars. The little "Century" piece strikes me deeper and deeper—all things considered it is one of the best—one of the most valuable things you ever penned—magnum in petto. If you think of it show this letter to Horace, want him to see the meter news

R M Bucke   See notes 2/2/90
