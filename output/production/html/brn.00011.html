 brn.00002.001_large.jpg 328 Mickle street Camden New Jersey U S America Feb:​ 11 '86 My dear K C

I send you the two Volumes, same mail with this,1 (same address as this note)—The price is one pound two shillings, which please remit me in post office order—I am ab't​ as usual of late, bodily disabled, but in good heart.

Walt Whitman  brn.00002.002_large.jpg

Correspondent:
Kenningale Robert Cook (1845–1886) was editor of the Dublin University Magazine, and he was married to popular Victorian novelist Mabel Collins (Marion Meade, Madame Blavatsky: The Woman Behind the Myth [New York: G.P. Putnam's Sons, 1980], 390). Cook was also the author of The Fathers of Jesus: A Study of the Lineage of the Christian Doctrine and Traditions, 2 vols. (London: K. Paul, Trench & Co., 1886).

Notes

  • 1. Cook ordered books from Whitman in 1876; see his letter of February 29, 1876. On April 23, 1877, he asked the poet's permission to print some verses in the Dublin University Magazine (see Horace Traubel, With Walt Whitman in Camden, Wednesday, August 29, 1888, 219). [back]
