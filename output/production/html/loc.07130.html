  30 Nov. 1890

[cut away] dear Walt, of the death of Thomas Jefferson and Horace tells me in the same letter that you are not feeling well yourself. I feel a good deal of anxiety about you, I know your wonderful constitution but for all that blows like this must produce their effect. You will not I know give way to depression more than you can help. I wish I could bear part of this shock for you. Do not forget that your life and health are very precious to many of us and try to bear up for our sake as well as for your own. I have had no account directly or indirectly yet as to how Dr. Mitchell found you or what he thought of you, neither do I know whether he was able
