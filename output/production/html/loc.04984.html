  Superintendent's Office. ASYLUM FOR THE INSANE LONDON. ONTARIO London, Ont., 13 April 1890

I have your cards of 8th and 9th Have had the inspector here the last two days and a lot of work and worry along with him (as usual)—this has kept me from writing—you speak of being "easier" but I feel very anxious about you and think I shall hurry up and see you soon, probably early in May. I did think of putting off my trip until the end of May but there are reasons why I had better go now—I might not be able to go at the end of May.

The weather here has turned warm, almost summer today—sun shining, birds singing—buds bursting out. No doubt it will be cold again in a day or two, too early for real spring yet

R M Bucke
