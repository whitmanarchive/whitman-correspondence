  Camden Saturday Afternoon July 21 '88

Rather easier to-day—am writing a little & at my proofs (the little new book "Nov: Boughs")—have just rec'd three letters, one from my English friend Ernest Rhys, friend of Herbert Gilchrist, and one from Dr Bucke—Nothing special, but I will enclose them all (as I believe you are entertain'd—like I am by such)—I wish you to send the three back to me in the envelope—write a line to tell how you all [got] back—I liked your visit—have enjoy'd the chicken—did you get back all right? did you get the money all right at the bank?—pleasant weather here to-day—cloudy—

Sunday noon July 22

Am sitting up by the window—a little headache, & heavy feeling—but I must take the thick with the thin—moderately cool & rainy—very bearable for July—I am comfortable—good spirits—

Walt Whitman
