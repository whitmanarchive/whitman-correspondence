  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 11 June 1891

Last night and today we have had a hard rain which was much needed. It is still dark and threatening. Your post card of 9th came to hand this morning and we were glad that you were no worse. H & I are hard at all sorts of W.W. plans and speculations. H. has got his Lippencott​ Lippincott​ "Round Table" piece in pretty good shape, it will be A.I.—We have pretty well planned our W.W. book also—H. will show you the list of pieces which are to go in it when he returns. It will all have to be approved by you. Tomorrow I think we shall all go for a 15 mile drive to Delaware—H., Anne, Mrs.B., my boy Maurice, self, & it should be splendid driving after the rain

Best love R M Bucke
