  Camden June 19 1890 My dear friend & all

It is a wonderful fine day, cool enough & I am feeling fairly—every thing going right (but of course steadily down hill)—Young George was here, & I was glad to see him—he looks well—I hear from Dr Bucke f'm Canada, & f'm Edw'd Carpenter—he is well—my friends the Smiths (of Germantown) are all settled in London & seem to like it—Herbert is still at Centreport Long Island—I get splendid letters f'm Col. Ingersoll & a noble book of his later pieces (I wish dear Harry could see them all—he would gloat over them)—I get out in wheel chair—am quite helpless & if I live much longer shall be blind—Love to you & George & Ed & Deb & all—this card is the dinner card for curiosity—

Walt Whitman

June 20

Another fine bright day—all ab't as usual—all well & quiet—best love & wishes to you all—
