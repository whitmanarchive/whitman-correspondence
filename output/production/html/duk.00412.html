March 12 1889 Bro Walt.

Han is uneasy for a postal. She is getting on quite well—makes good bread—weather is cold, to night.

I had quite a long conference to day with Revd I. J. Bliss, rectory Episcopal church—he expressed great sympathy for me; in my straitend circumstances, and placed a 5 dollar bill, in my hand, as he has done   once before, this winter, which got me 1/2 ton of coal and provisions

I shall have better luck after a time.

I am on the petite​ petit​ jury, commencing April 2 dollars per day. I would much rather paint, could I sell my pictures.

Saw Mrs Tyler in street, to day

Kindly I remain— Charles
