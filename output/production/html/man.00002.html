  Asylum for the Insane, London, Jan​ 19th 1880 My dear Walt

I am going to ask a great favor of you—I want you to write me a sketch of your interior life—especially in relation to the conception and elaboration of "Leaves of Grass" The germanancy and path of such a product as "Leaves of Grass" is a psycological expression almost unique in the history of the race and some record of it ought to remain if possible—I need not explain any further what I want from you for you will understand at once what I mean and you must surely have  often thought of putting it upon record

I hope that you will take this matter into serious and favourable consideration

And I am Faithfully yours R M Bucke
