  Medical Superintendent's Office INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 13 Oct. 1891

I have your letter of 10th I fear you are not having a good time and I guess this change in the weather from hot to almost cold will not be favorable to you—I wish you would ask Longaker if he does not think a steam bath (with alcohol lamp) would be good for you—say about once—or even twice a week? Here all goes well—bright, crisp weather and all in good health—pretty busy, but that does no harm—Inspector is to be here tomorrow or next day—I am about half through my annual report—I hear nothing from you or Horace about the new L. of G.—What are you doing with it? Send me a line as often as possible—I cannot do much for you but I am here to do what I can

With best Love R M Bucke
