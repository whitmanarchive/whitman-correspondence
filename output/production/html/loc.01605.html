  The Daily Graphic January 19, 1874 Walt Whitman:

We enclose the proof of your second article; but would much prefer that you should get the copy strait​ straight​ the first time. Such very heavy corrections in proof cause great embarrassment and expense in a newspaper office. We are quite willing to give proofs for verbal corrections; but we do not like to reset and readjust so largely. So please have a care to fix your copy correctly before sending it in.

Yours very truly DG Croly
