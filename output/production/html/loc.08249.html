Camden 3½ P M Nov: 1 '90

Have been out in wheel chair for hour & half, & enjoy'd it—all goes as usual—sunny to day cool—I send slip of "Old Poets" —Horace here last evn'g—he will delay sending back the signatured books for reasons—Partly promised the N[orth] A[merican] Rev[iew] I w'd give them a 2d article ab't American Literature (enormous name!) or something that sort—& I think I will get at it, but it must be a rehash of what I have already said—still grip—bad head, pains &c:—am sitting here by stove in my den, same as ever—Horace will send you Morris's piece (contra-Ingersoll) in American—I cannot find fault with M sharply (as H does) while I thank & side with & even espouse Ing-ism & Ing: himself—(he has treated me so splendidly too)—

Walt Whitman
