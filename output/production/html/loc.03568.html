  10 Spencer Hill. Wimbledon London. March 8th Dear Walt

After a time of much anxiety about you the papers gave better accounts & we may hope that your last word of promise & encouragment has not yet been spoken to men. I got from Traubel the L. of G. of 1892—a noble   volume & just such a one as I have wished to possess. My friend Standish O'Grady asks me to address a letter to you. Take, with his, my words of sympathy in your late heavy trial, & of unalterable homage & love. You have given such faith & courage to so many souls that I doubt not your own ride   high over all surges of pain & physical dissolution. And I pray that strength & cheer may reach you from those deepest sources which you have opened up for others & set flowing eternally. Goodbye dear Walt, may God bless and keep you in any and every lot that may be yours.

Truly your friend T.W. Rolleston
