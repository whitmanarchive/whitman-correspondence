Washington, July 21, 1870. My dear friend,

I have just received your letter of the 7th inst. I appreciate your kindness in the matter of the poem. I send herewith a verbatim copy of Emerson's note, as requested.

Nothing very new or special with me, these days. I am well as usual—am still employed in the Attorney General's Office. A new edition of my book will be printed this fall, with another small volume in prose. You shall have early copies, may-be in sheets.

Farewell for the present. I send you my love—Write whenever you can.

Walt Whitman
