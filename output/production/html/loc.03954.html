  Nov.​ 9, 1877. Dear sir.

I am well and hope you the same I would be very glad to see you as i did expect to come down [damage] you to day or i would of wrote sooner. and if nothing hapins​ happens​ I will be down next wensday​ Wednesday​ night and stay until thursday night I would like very much to see you you must rite​ write​ right a way​ away​ and tell me how you are a getting along I would like to be with you all the time if i could i would like very much to go with you on your trip to washington. we are very buisy​ busy​ husking just now and will be fore​ for​ a few days and we will have nothing to do for a while)

 

I am in a hurry i cant rite​ write​ any so it is good by​ goodbye​ i hope you well

Elmer E. Stafford Glendale To Walt. Whitman NJ
