 Camden, [N. J.] Dec. 29, '73. A. K. Butts, Dear Sir,

Yours of 26th rec'd. It looks like something beginning to be done. About Piper's bill you can wait till you go personally to Boston.

I have written to O'Kane to–day, & I hope you will have no further difficulty in getting the books—I have requested him to send word to you to come & get them. If we get started in the way we talked of, (& I have no doubt we shall,) & satisfaction is felt on both sides, it is certainly my intention that you shall have actual & complete control of the sales—& all supplies.
