  Camden, N.J. Dear Sir;—

I am an earnest collector of the autographs of prominent men; and would be greatly pleased to place your autograph among those of some grand poets, such as I have among my treasured list. As those of Whittier, Holmes, and Lord Tennyson, and may I soon place your autograph among those who you are worthy to be placed.

Dear Sir, Oliver W. Holmes kindly wrote for me his poem, "The Last Leaf"; and Tennyson wrote for me the first verse of his beautiful "Break Break Break." Would you kindly do likewise. How I would treasure a poem from "the good gray poet."
