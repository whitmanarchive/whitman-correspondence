Camden— April 21, '87

. . . I go over this afternoon at urgent request of my friend R. P. Smith—to some quarters in Arch St. provided for me, where I believe I am to be sculp'd by St. Gaudens, the sculptor. I rec'd $600. for my N. Y. reading. Andrew Carnegie (thro' Gilder) paid $350 for his box. . . . I have eaten a good breakfast with zest.

Walt Whitman
