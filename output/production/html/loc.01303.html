  letter sent to R W Colles, Dublin | Nov: 18 '1886

Yours of 31st and 1st rec'd. I send the two Vols: of '76 Ed'n. I should accept with thanks from your hands any contribution from my Irish friends (Prof. Edw'd Dowden has already contributed liberally, & should not be solicited any further) Take leisure and ease ab't it & let it amount to what it may—or naught at all, if Destiny so disposes.

(tr to last Send me word if the two Vols. reach you safely. The price is £2, which please remit me by p. o. order.)

Yes, I should like to see the article in the Quarterly. My late bad spell of sickness seems to have passed over—I was out driving yesterday the three sunny midday hours, & enjoyed them much.
