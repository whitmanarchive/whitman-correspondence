  328 Mickle Street Camden New Jersey March 4 '85 My dear friends

Your letter comforts & touches me deeply, & I am not sure but it w'd be a good arrangement not only for me, but all 'round—But for the present I shall keep on here—Since you was here, Alma, I have had a friend move in, Mrs. Davis, strong & hearty & good natured, a widow, young enough, furnishes me my meals, & takes good care—I am feeling quite well for me as I write this. —Soon as you get this write me how John is getting along—Last Saturday's Critic has  a piece about my home which may interest you—I have been under the weather myself for nearly a fortnight, but am now all right—I shall never forget your kindness & generosity to me—I am in good spirits as I finish this, feeling better to-day than for some time past—Love to Al and May & all

Walt Whitman
