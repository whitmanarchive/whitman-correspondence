  Medical Superintendent's Office. INSANE ASYLUM LONDON ONTARIO 28 Jan 1891

Yours of 24th to hand yesterday. Was too much occupied to write. Sorry (and disgusted) to hear of the action of Scribner's but "such is life." Nothing at all new here. Charming winter weather and good roads (tho' no sleighing). My shoulder is all right as far as being comfortable goes but is not good for much to use yet. Am glad you like H's piece on W.W.—as I told you before I think very highly of it. We are ready going to have meters at last—the first dozen will be done this week or early next—after that we can make them as fast as we want them—next thing will be to start manufacturing on your side

Affectionately R M Bucke
