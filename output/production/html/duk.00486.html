  2 aug '91 Sunday Morning—

A glorious day outside—Han is resting quietly on the sofa, Lou had so nicely had repaired. She had a serious time yesterday—on the floor with pain. I obtained a nurse, for my time was occupied in procuring remedies—all day—until allmost​ almost​ night. I was on the run, without food sufficient to sustain me. I was quite broken down—famished and most irritable—completely prostrated myself. Han must have her mattress made over. The woman who attemped to make up her bed—could not succeed satisfactorially. It will cost but 5.50—will be called for at 9 o'clock   and returned at 5 as good as new. It cost me 20 dollars cash, in Myrtle Av, Brooklyn.

I'm not selling any paintings yet I have some fine ones, could I but get away them—to Adirondack, but that is impossible—Han says you will, or can make it all right. You must not criticise me, Walt—Mother Whitman had great faith, sympathy, and respect—for me. I have improved this property more than 1000$—by my personal industry.

Yours truly Charlie

Our noble Friend and Brother Walt—allways​ always​ , and forever to be remembered.
