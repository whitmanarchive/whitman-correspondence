  Aug 24 1871 Dear Sir:

When I wrote to you yesterday I quite forgot to mention that Mr. Swinburne had for a long time been very much concerned that not knowing your address he had been unable to send you a copy of his "Songs before Sunrise". As I think it possible that       by this time you may have got the book I send you one of the special copies printed on fine paper, of which only 25 were struck off and shall feel much gratified by your acceptance of it.

Believe me Dear Sir Yours faithfully, F. S. Ellis To Walt Whitman Esqr. Ellis London
